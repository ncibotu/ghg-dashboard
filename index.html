Methodology & Caveats
Grid-sensitive actions (EV, green electricity, heat pump, heat pump water heater) scale savings proportionally with each country's grid intensity: saving = US_baseline Ã— (country_grid / US_grid). A cleaner grid means less additional benefit from electrification because the baseline electricity is already low-carbon.

Diet-sensitive actions (plant-based diet, cut beef) scale with a meat consumption index (meat_idx) normalized to US=1.0, derived from FAO Food Balance Sheets. A lower meat_idx means the baseline diet is already lower in animal products, so the gain from further reduction is smaller in absolute terms.

Household baselines are approximate. Per-capita national GHG figures (EDGAR/IEA) include industrial processes and agriculture at national level; actual household consumption footprints vary significantly within countries. Figures shown are best estimates for a typical household and should be treated as indicative.

Currency conversion uses approximate spot rates as of 2024-2025. Local cost ranges (cost_lo / cost_hi) are rough estimates; actual costs depend on market conditions, incentives, and individual circumstances.

Relevance ratings (High / Medium / Low / Variable) are qualitative assessments based on country-level context (car ownership rates, grid intensity, dietary patterns, infrastructure) and do not represent quantitative probability estimates. All data should be treated as indicative and for educational purposes.
